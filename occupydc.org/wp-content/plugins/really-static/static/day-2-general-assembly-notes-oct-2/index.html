Day 2: General Assembly Notes (Oct 2)

October 3rd, 2011 · Bob · General Assembly 3 comments

Fourth GA Meeting, 10/2/11, 6:00 p.m.

Facilitator: Philip
Assistant Facilitator: Caty

First Agenda Item: Status update

  • Maury gives status update: we got mentioned on Press TV (Iranian state tv); we get Tweeted at or about at a rate of about 8 times per minute; Occupied cities are reporting turnouts in the multiple hundreds and Occupy Wall Street is seeing thousands; Chris Morgan is doing a radio interview with Red Eye Radio.

 

Second Agenda Item: Committee updates

-Corinne delivers first update from Fundraising:

  • They had raised about $140 since this morning.
  • Working on a tool for online donations and approaching non-profits for larger expenses like a legal fund, tarps.
  • Called meeting of Finance committee after GA.

-Food Committee delivers update:

  • Got delivery this morning from a local church (including a cake that said “Congratulations!”).
  • Have an anonymous dedicated source of food.
  • Temple Sinai will be donating food.

-Medical delivers update:

  • *they have a new member who is a former EMT.
  • snnounce pepper spray education class after GA.

-Documentation and Tech delivers update

  • In need of some kind of wifi capability.
  • Creating a widget that will aggregate all media – tweets, videos, photos, blog posts, etc – that is tagged with a compendium of #occupy tags.
  • Moving servers and revamping the website.

-Sanitation delivers update:

  • Need extra helpers for after hours.

-Outreach delivers update

  • Talking with local community leaders who have interest in what we’re doing

Arts and Entertainment delivers update:

  • Collecting new chants for marches, ideally original ones.

 

Agenda Item 3: Storing our nonperishable’s, food, signs, etc overnight

  • nearby organization will let us store them there tonight
  • working on finding a dedicated place

Agenda Item 4: March debrief from Ali

  • Marched to White House, circled back past AFL-CIO, who have pledged support; got lots of tourist attention, of passed out fliers.
  • Wall Street group marches at 9 a.m. and 4:30 pm, for opening and closing bell; suggested we coordinate times in solidarity with them and other occupy groups – some consensus
    • Point is made that the 6:00 hour is when most lobbyists, interns, House and Senate, etc leave work, so maybe we should march during their rush hour.
    • Motion is made to march at 9:00 a.m. and 6:00 p.m., could change if needed – approved.
  • Suggestion is made to march to IMF.
  • Suggestion is made to march on K St rather than to White House.

 

Agenda Item 5: Announcement from Nicole

  • Has extensive experience with collecting detailed information about money in politics (campaign finance, etc)
  • Can do a variety of educational work with anyone interested (teach-in, walking tours, etc)

On that note, recommendation is made that that kind of information would be good flier material – who we are, GA times, links to information sources for occupy movements, and a couple very strong statistics – general agreement

-Proposal is made that we form some kind of group to work on messaging

–Counter is made that we should continue to avoid too much bureaucratic organization; we all should participate in processes like messaging

—Facilitator proposes we come back to this conversation during our allotted 30 minute block at the end of the meeting – approved

 

Agenda Item 6: Announcement from Jim

  • a couple hundred more people may be coming around noon on Tuesday
  •  On that note, a suggestion is made that we hold morning GA at 11:00 a.m. rather than noon, so we can use lunchtime as an outreach opportunity

-point is raised that if we meet at noon, those of us who work in the area can come on lunch breaks

-suggestion is made that we could hold GA meetings after marches

–suggestion is made we hold them at 8:00 p.m. so that more people can attend

—concern that 8:00 is too late to start a meeting

—comments in favor of various meeting times and march times are made—-

—-Assistant facilitator proposes that in the interest of simplicity the group first either approve or reject the idea to meet after marches – approve

—Assistant facilitator proposes that we second decide between a 5:00, 6:00 and 7:00 p.m. march time

-proposal to march at 5:00 p.m., hold GA at 6 p.m. – approved

 

Agenda Item 7: 30-minute messaging conversation

-idea: we’re here for the 99%, and it should be that simple

–idea: we should keep in mind that many of us are here because we can be, and that we stand for people who can’t be

-idea: take the Wall Street list as a starting point, go from there; no reason to start from scratch and spend time going back and forth on things for which there’s already a framework from which to work

–idea: all occupy cities are unique and each group’s more specific focuses reflect that; politics are in DC, we could work with preexisting ideas but gear them toward political issues

—counterpoint is raised that Wall Street’s list came from many days of work; we should respect that process and have the experience of going through it ourselves

Assistant facilitator gave 10-minute notice to facilitator; facilitator proposes we address an issue raised earlier, that had general, unofficial consensus, about having a cycle of facilitators at each GA; proposes any interested parties can talk after the GA – approved

Meeting adjourned
