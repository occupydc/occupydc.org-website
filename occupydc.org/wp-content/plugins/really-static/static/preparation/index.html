Preparation

September 30th, 2011 · Bob · 13 comments

DC Protest Guide: http://localhostr.com/files/3qAwGqv/dcprotestguide.pdf

When attending protests it’s an good idea to prepare yourself. Here below you can find information on what to bring and legal advice.

What to Bring

  1. Food: brings bars, bread, fruit, etc!
  2. Water! Make sure to bring bottled water.
  3. Blankets, towels, raincoats, tarps, ropes, plastic zip bags to protect electronics, etc.
  4. Dress warm it can get cold: http://www.weather.com/weather/today/USDC0001
  5. Fully charged cellphone and power adapter.
  6. Medical/First Aid supplies.
  7. Money, insurance card (if you have one), and an ID.
  8. Banners, flyers, posters, etc!
  9. Cardboard, Sharpies, pens, pencils, paint, chalk, etc!!!
  10. Digital camera (video recorder).
  11. Books, board games, etc – got to keep yourself entertained sooner or later!

Do NOT Bring

  1. Any sort of weapons including knifes and pepper spray.
  2. Illegal substances.
  3. Alcohol or any other intoxicants.
  4. Laser Pointers.

  6. Lark on October 1st, 2011 at 8:49 pm #

    Have valid photo govt-issued ID, cash in a very safe place, memorize phone numbers. I’d also suggest carrying a few bottles of water, not for drinking, for skin rinsing. Act peaceably. Don’t argue. Obey reasonable orders. Help each other. Call police Sir or Ma’am, be polite. Most police are working to support families and need their jobs and are potential allies, not enemies.

    Be aware that many protestors who try to lead crowds into bad action may be provocateurs, trying to create a situation that will make the protests look bad in the media.

    Look like, act, and be someones neighbor, friend, kid, cousin… we are in this not for ourselves but for each other against the greedheads.

    Realize that if there is an arrest sweep, citing your rights at that time will probably not stop you being swept, and may lead to being treated physically as if you are resisting arrest and then charges. For the police, a sweep is a time of heightened fear/energy and they aren’t thinking with the forebrain.

    Go along. Be peaceable. Be safe. Help others be safe.

    Please don’t dress in protective gear. NO helmets, NO goggles, NO protection. Shoes are rather better than sandals, more toe protection, sneakers are excellent. Dressing to protect yourself is provocative. Provocation is just about guaranteed to amp things up and elicit the very violence you are trying to protect yourself from. Dressing up elicits a reaction. Dress for the reaction you wish to cause.

    The point is to look normal, recognizable, like a great neighbor, like someone with whom middle America can associate and be friends with. Dress like you respect those people who don’t yet know they are allies with a common cause, dress like you respect the cause.
